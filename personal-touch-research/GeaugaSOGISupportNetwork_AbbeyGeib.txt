========================================
BUSINESS DESCRIPTION
========================================
Geauga SOGI Support Network (GSSN) was established through collaborative efforts of parents, LGBTQIA individuals, and professional counselors serving this population. Officially registered as an independent, 501(c)3 non-profit organization in April 2020, during the pandemic.

Mission: Engaging individuals, families, friends and Geauga Community to embrace Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity through collaboration, education and advocacy.

Services: Monthly support groups for middle school, high school, and LGBTQIA adults and parents of LGBTQIA. Educational programs, overview training, and events (such as Pride in Geauga). All services are free and open to Geauga County residents and surrounding communities.

Website: www.geaugasogi.org

3. **Pride in Geauga Events**: Successfully hosting Pride events in rural, conservative Geauga County - significant achievement for LGBTQ+ visibility in area not traditionally known for LGBTQ+ acceptance.

5. **Multi-Generational Support**: Offering separate groups for middle school, high school, adults, and parents shows understanding of different developmental and support needs across age groups.

6. **Rural LGBTQ+ Service**: Featured in The Buckeye Flame (November 2021) for "aiming to create a more inclusive Geauga County" - recognition of important work in underserved rural area.

========================================
PAIN POINTS
========================================

1. **Funding Sustainability**: As nonprofit offering all free services, securing consistent funding through grants and donations is ongoing challenge, especially in rural area without major corporate donors.

2. **Awareness in Conservative Area**: Geauga County is more conservative than urban Cleveland. Reaching LGBTQ+ individuals and families who may be closeted or unaware of services requires sensitive, strategic outreach.

3. **Volunteer Recruitment & Retention**: Support groups, events, and education programs require trained volunteers. Recruiting and retaining qualified volunteers in rural area can be challenging.

4. **Youth Safety & Parental Permission**: Reaching LGBTQ+ youth who may not be out to parents or whose parents are unsupportive creates complex safety and legal considerations.

5. **Limited Professional Resources**: While founded with professional counselor involvement, sustaining access to mental health professionals, educators, and trained facilitators in rural area requires ongoing recruitment.

========================================
COMMON GROUND OPPORTUNITIES
========================================

1. **Rural LGBTQ+ Service Gap**: GSSN serves critical underserved population. Many LGBTQ+ organizations focus on urban areas; rural LGBTQ+ people face isolation, limited resources, and less acceptance.

2. **Youth Mental Health Crisis**: LGBTQ+ youth have significantly higher rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide. GSSN's support groups address urgent mental health need.

3. **Family Acceptance Focus**: Research shows family acceptance is strongest protective factor for LGBTQ+ youth wellbeing. GSSN's parent support groups facilitate this critical acceptance.

4. **Community Education**: Educational programs and training help create more inclusive Geauga County, benefiting entire community through increased understanding and reduced discrimination.

5. **Grassroots Organizing**: GSSN's collaborative, community-driven model resonates with values of local empowerment and neighbors helping neighbors.
