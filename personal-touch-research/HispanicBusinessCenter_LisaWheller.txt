========================================
BUSINESS DESCRIPTION
========================================
The Northeast Ohio Hispanic Center for Economic Development (NEOHCED), known as the Hispanic Business Center (HBC), is committed to equitable asset building in Cleveland's Latino and other underserved communities by providing economic and entrepreneurial technical assistance, training, and advocacy.

HBC assists all business owners in achieving their goals - regardless of age, race, income level, gender or company size. The center is home to Northeast Ohio's only Latino Small Business Development Center (SBDC) and is a Black woman owned business based in Cleveland.

Located at 2511 Clark Avenue in La Villa Hispana (the neighborhood with the highest density of Latinos in the State of Ohio), HBC provides free, confidential, in-depth, one-on-one counseling and low- or no-cost training in Spanish.

========================================
PAIN POINTS
========================================

1. **Community Awareness**: Many Latino entrepreneurs, especially recent immigrants, may not know HBC exists or that free, Spanish-language business support is available.

2. **Funding Sustainability**: As nonprofit providing free services, securing consistent funding from grants, government contracts, and donations is ongoing challenge.

3. **Serving Underserved**: Latino entrepreneurs often face barriers to capital, systemic discrimination, and lack of business networks. HBC works with clients starting from disadvantaged position.

4. **Capacity Constraints**: With only Latino SBDC in Northeast Ohio, demand likely exceeds capacity. Serving all who need help requires staff, funding, and resources.

5. **Economic Development in Underinvested Neighborhood**: Clark-Fulton / La Villa Hispana area faces economic challenges. Creating thriving business district requires sustained investment and partnerships.
