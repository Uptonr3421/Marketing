========================================
BUSINESS DESCRIPTION
========================================
Everybody Cycle is a locally owned and queer managed indoor cycling studio located in Cleveland's Edgewater Neighborhood at 11606 Detroit Avenue, Cleveland, OH 44102. The studio offers 45-minute indoor cycling classes designed as a safe space to grow your spinning practice, where every identity, every ability, and everyBODY is welcome.

Everybody Cycle is both LGBTQ-friendly and LGBTQ-owned, emphasizing accessibility and inclusion. They offer their first ride free and operate with a philosophy of "live your truth" in a mirror-free, body-positive environment.

Phone: (216) 352-5490
Website: everybodycycle.com

========================================
RECENT WINS/NEWS/ACHIEVEMENTS
========================================

1. **Innovative Accessibility Equipment**: Everybody Cycle designed and manufactured a piece of equipment (patent pending), only available at their studio, which transforms a commercial indoor cycling bike into an accessible arm crank. Riders can access this from their wheelchair or a padded seat - revolutionary for making cycling accessible to people with mobility differences.

2. **Mirror-Free Studio**: They're the only indoor cycling studio in the area with a mirror-free workout area to avoid triggering body consciousness and support body positivity at every size.

3. **Health At Every Size Training**: Staff undergo specialized "health at every size" training to create a truly size-inclusive space, going beyond lip service to meaningful inclusion.

4. **Confidence-Building Programming**: Regularly held trainings specifically designed to build riders' confidence, especially for those new to cycling or intimidated by traditional fitness spaces.

5. **Stonewall Sports Cleveland Partnership**: Hosted charity events supporting Stonewall Sports Cleveland (December 2024), showing active involvement in LGBTQ+ sports and community organizations.

6. **Gender-Inclusive Facilities**: Provides gender-inclusive restroom, lockers, and seating areas.

========================================
PAIN POINTS
========================================

1. **Competing with Boutique Fitness Chains**: National chains like SoulCycle, Cyclebar, and Orangetheory have marketing budgets and brand recognition. Standing out as local, independent studio requires compelling differentiation.

2. **Overcoming Fitness Anxiety**: Target audience includes people intimidated by traditional gyms/fitness spaces. Requires marketing that reassures, educates, and builds confidence before first visit.

3. **Visibility & Awareness**: Many Clevelanders may not know Everybody Cycle exists or understand what makes it different from other cycling studios. Need strategic local marketing and community partnerships.

4. **Sustaining Class Fill Rates**: Indoor cycling classes need consistent attendance to be financially viable. Requires building loyal membership base and attracting new riders regularly.

5. **Communicating Accessibility Features**: The patent-pending arm crank equipment is revolutionary, but people who would benefit need to know it exists. Requires targeted outreach to disability community and adaptive sports organizations.
